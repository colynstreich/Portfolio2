Course Goals


1. Consider technologys role in acts of making

Techonolgys role in the acts of making has dramtically influenced what I can do in digital media. With the easy accesability of technology, it allowed me to create more complex and interactive digital experiences through new media platforms. For example, I can now create interactive websites, like my webtext design project, through the accesability given to me from technology.

2. Embrace experimentation

Through these projects, I have embraced experimentation in digital media. I have explored new forms of media I had no previous knowledge of, such as interactive webtexts and multimedia presentations. This expirenmentation allowed me to think creatively and learn new skills for the ideas I had. For example my web text project allowed me to think in an expiremntal way to discover how I wanted to convey the reading to others. Through expirmentation in the projects I have learned to be more flexible and open to new ideas and approaches in digital media.

3.Engage with others in a workshop environment

Workshop has been a valuable expirence allowing me to engage with other students ideas and creativity, but also allow me to troubleshoot and learn new ways of doing things in digital media. For example when I struggled to upload my files into website format I was able to get help from my peers and learn new techniques to improve my workflow.

4. Practice revision within and across media forms

These projects and presentations made me much more confident in my ability to revise and improve my work across different media forms. This is because I learned how my peers also run into challenges and how they overcame them, which helped me develop more comfort with revision and improvement processes.

5. Learn to present your work

The expression of my peers issues related to their projects made me more aware of how to effectively communicate my own work and the challenges I faced. This experience helped me develop stronger presentation skills and become more comfortable expressing mistakes and learning from them. For example I struggled with my webtext design, but found that presenting it to others helped me identify areas for improvement and gain confidence in my work.

Course Goals

1. Consider technology’s role in 3cts of making

Techonology plays a big role in the 3 acts of making. In the first stage the technology allows the creator to form ideas and organize them. In the second stage, technology helps in the execution and refinement of the ideas. In the third stage, technology aids in the dissemination and sharing of the final product. As I began my collage project I researched ideas on pinterest. I then began to play around with my program and lasty I made the idea concrete and ready to be submitted.


2. Embrace experimentation

Within media you have the opportunity to experiment with different techniques and approaches. This allows you to push the boundaries of your creativity and discover new ways of expressing your ideas. For example coming into the class with an open mind and willingness to try new things I realized the importance of experimentation. When designing my collage I reailed that this new program was extremely difficult to master. However, I persisted and learned a lot about composition and how to play around with different effects.


3.Engage with others in a workshop environment

Engaging with others in a workshop environment allows for collaborative learning and the exchange of ideas. This allows for a better understanding of the subject matter and the development of new perspectives.I did this by asking how my peers approached similar projects and how they chose to edit their processes. This helped in my editing and refining of my work in my podcast


4. Practice revision within and across media forms

Practicing revision within and across media forms is essential for developing a strong portfolio. It allows you to refine your ideas and improve the quality of your work. I used this idea by looking at my peers projects before the deadline. This helped me identify areas for improvement and refine my final product. This was especially helpful in ensuring that my photo essay.


5. Learn to present your work

Presenting your work effectively is crucial for communicating your ideas and receiving feedback. I learned to present my work by practicing my explanations and receiving constructive criticism from my peers in our group discussions. For example how I explained my reasoning behind design choices within my collage and why I decided to fade the text and gradually have more pictures to the right.
